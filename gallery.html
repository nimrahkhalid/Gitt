Global Heritages Gallery

The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is one of the most iconic structures in the world, representing China's ancient history and defense strategy against invasions.

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat in Cambodia is the largest religious monument in the world, built during the Khmer Empire and dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal in India is a symbol of love and one of the most stunning examples of Mughal architecture, known for its white marble structure.

Machu Picchu

Located in Peru, Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city that was built in the 15th century and is known for its sophisticated dry-stone construction and panoramic views.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, located in Paris, France, is a wrought-iron lattice tower that was constructed as the entrance arch to the 1889 World's Fair and is now one of the most recognized landmarks in the world.

Pyramids of Giza

The Pyramids of Giza, located in Egypt, are ancient monuments that represent the pinnacle of Egyptian architecture and were built as tombs for the Pharaohs during the Fourth Dynasty.

Christ the Redeemer

Located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, Christ the Redeemer is a towering statue of Jesus Christ that stands as a symbol of Christianity and the city of Rio de Janeiro.

Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef in Australia is the largest coral reef system in the world, known for its biodiversity and its importance to marine life.

Table Mountain

Table Mountain in Cape Town, South Africa, is a flat-topped mountain that is one of the New7Wonders of Nature and offers panoramic views of the city and coastline.

The Colosseum

A symbol of ancient Greek civilization, the Acropolis includes the Parthenon and other classical structures that date back to the 5th century BC.

Stonehenge

Located in England, Stonehenge is a prehistoric monument that consists of a ring of standing stones, the origin and purpose of which remain a mystery.

The Acropolis of Athens

A symbol of ancient Greek civilization, the Acropolis includes the Parthenon and other classical structures that date back to the 5th century BC.
